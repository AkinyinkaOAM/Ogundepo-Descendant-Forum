BRIEF HISTORY OF THE OGUNDEPO’S FAMILY

Ogundepo was a warrior that hailed from sigidi Compound in Aboto town of Erin ile in Ilorin kwara state.
His great grandfather was ikueji while his grandfather was Apati. Apati has two sons called Egbeolu and Orowusi.
Egbeolu was the father of Ogundepo, Lajube, Arodou and Oluwusi.

OGUNDEPO

As a warrior, Ogundepo entered Ibadan around 1828 like his other warriors with his followers, slaves and few of
his family. On his way coming to Ibadan, He met an old man called Opeagbe who was also a warrior from Ogbomosho
land at Aroje kan. Both Opeagbe and Ogundepo entered Ibadan the same day and stayed with a man called Ojo. Ojo
invited his Ifa divination officer to discover who are his visitors that is; (Ogundepo and Opeagbe). It was
discovered that Ogundepo was a warrior/ fighter. Ifa divination officer then told Ojo that ikuree (death) and
since then Ojo has been given a nickname called Ojo’kure. After a while, Opeagbe and Ogundepo
decided to have their own separate settlement and they were sent to settle at an area called Odo – Okun side in Ibadan.
Opeagbe stayed at upper bushy area called Omiyale now because of his age while Ogundepo decided to settle down towards
odo-okun bushy side. Ogundepo was known to be rascal (as it was written in the history of Ibadan) by Samuel Johnson) and stayed
there and joined other warriors whenever needed arises to protect Ibadan land against war. One of the evidences to show
his rascality (Ogundepo) was that: Olatunbosun ridiculed Baale Orowusi and this was narraated to Ogundepo because as at that time,
Ogundepo was at war front when he returned and heard the incident he went ahead to Olatunbosun’s compound and beheaded him and
took his head to Orowusi’s house. On the 22nd of May, 1871, OGEDENGBE who supported the crowning of Prince ODIGBADIGBA with the
assistance from Balogun Ajobo to became Owa of Ilesha illegally, (that was, without the approval of BAALE AND HIS IBADAN CHIEFS).
This led to raged of war by the Ibadan Chiefs against Ilesha people and in which the perpetrators were arrested and brought to Ibadan
and they (perpetrators) were detained at OGUNDEPO QUARTERS. The followings are the some of the wars in which Ogundepo participated
up to the time that Yoruba war ended in1893 by signing the end of Yoruba war treaty during the reign of baale Fijabi:
Some of the wars HE participated are:
1) Osogbo war 1840
2) Ijaye war 1842-1845
3) Ijebu Ere war 1862-1864
4) Jalumi war 1878
5) Kiriji/Ekiti parapo and many more.

OGUNDEPO AS AN IBADAN CHIEF

Ogundepo was one of the signatories to stoppage of inter tribal wars and slave trade in Yoruba land. The agreement/treaty was signed at Ibadan
on the 15th of August, 1893, under the Acting governor George Chardin Denton. The names of the Baale and chiefs, who were signatories to the treaty
